NELSON MANDELA


Born: July 18, 1918, Mvezo, South Africa
Died: December 5, 2013, Johannesburg, South Africa


INTRODUCTION

Nelson Mandela was a remarkable individual who had a profound impact on both South Africa and the world. He was born in 1918 in Mvezo, Transkei, and became a political activist at a young age.Nelson Mandela was an influential figure in the fight for democracy and human rights. He was imprisoned for 27 years, but eventually became the president of South Africa. He was awarded the Nobel Peace Prize in 1993 for his work on behalf of the people of South Africa.He fought for human rights and against apartheid, which was a government system that segregated people based on race.He is now considered one of the most influential figures in history. He left an incredible legacy. Mandela was a powerful voice for social justice, fighting for human rights and democracy worldwide. He is considered one of the greatest leaders in history and his influence is still felt today. Read about his amazing life story and see what he accomplished.Mandela’s role as president was not without its challenges he faced many struggles including years of imprisonment on Robben Island but his legacy is unquestioned. After his presidency ended in 1999, Mandela devoted himself to helping the poor and disadvantaged around the world through his non-profit organization, The Nelson Mandel Foundation. Today, he is widely recognized for his work with democracy and human rights.

Bioraphy

Mandela was a prominent anti-apartheid revolutionary, political leader, and philanthropist who served as the first black president of South Africa. His life and struggle against apartheid, as well as his leadership in the post-apartheid era, make for an inspiring biography. Here is a detailed account of Nelson Mandela's life: Early Life: Nelson Rolihlahla Mandela was born on July 18, 1918, in the small village of Mvezo in Umtata, then part of South Africa's Cape Province. His father, Gadla Henry Mphakanyiswa, was a chief, and his mother, Nosekeni Fanny, was the third of his father's four wives. Education and Law Career: Mandela attended primary school in Qunu and then the Clarkebury Boarding Institute. He later enrolled at Healdtown, a prestigious Wesleyan secondary school. In 1941, he moved to Johannesburg to escape a forced arranged marriage and found work as a night watchman while completing his bachelor's degree by correspondence at the University of South Africa. Mandela pursued a law degree at the University of Witwatersrand and later studied law at the University of Fort Hare. He became a lawyer and opened a law firm with Oliver Tambo, providing legal assistance to black South Africans who were discriminated against by the apartheid regime. Anti-Apartheid Activism: Mandela joined the African National Congress (ANC) in 1943, which was initially a non-violent organization advocating for civil rights.
